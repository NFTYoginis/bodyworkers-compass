# The Four Maps — and why there are four, not three

This researcher reads the body through **four maps.** Each is a different
*coordinate system* laid over the same physical terrain. They are not the same
map, and the skill is not collapsing them into one — it's knowing how to move a
finding between them without smuggling one map's claims into another.

Read this alongside `evidence-floor.md` (which gives the tier of each) and
`register-discipline.md` (which gives the exact language for linking them).

---

## The 3 → 4 reconciliation

The Joint Dialogue Method, as taught, names **three** maps:

| JDM's name | The map | The question it answers |
|---|---|---|
| Structural | **Anatomy Trains** (myofascial lines) | *How* — the physical chain of pull |
| Energetic / emotional | **Meridian circuits + Sen lines** | *Why / Where* — the functional story, the line of flow |
| Introspective | Kinesthetic awareness (the client's felt experience) | the lived sense of it |

This researcher splits and re-counts those into **four maps of the body's
structure**, plus separates the energetic map's two very different traditions:

1. **Joint / skeletal-muscular anatomy** — the fourth map, and the most
   evidence-solid. JDM treats this as the obvious substrate; we name it
   explicitly because it is the Tier-1 ground everything else is checked against.
2. **Anatomy Trains** — myofascial lines (Myers).
3. **TCM / meridian** — the acupuncture-channel map.
4. **Thai Sen lines** — the Thai lineage map.

We pull TCM and Sen apart (JDM groups them as "energetic") because they are
*different traditions with different evidence situations*: the TCM channel map
has a real, published *location* correlation to fascia (Langevin); the Sen map is
transmitted lineage knowledge. Blurring them would hide exactly the register
difference this tool exists to keep visible.

> The introspective / kinesthetic dimension (the client's own felt sense) is not
> a fourth structural map — it's the *mode* of the JDM dialogue. It lives in
> `vocabulary-bridge.md` (the Four Words sensation ladder), not here.

## Map 1 — Joint / skeletal-muscular anatomy (Tier 1)

The Western biomedical map: bones, joints, muscles, ligaments, nerves, and their
lever arms and load paths. Lumbar spine, sacroiliac joint, hip; the erectors, the
hamstrings, the glutes.

- **What it explains:** mechanical load and its consequences — how a short
  hamstring shifts load into the low back, how the SI joint shares forces with the
  hip.
- **Claim type:** testable, measured, uncontested.
- **Register:** state it as fact. This is the ground truth the other maps are
  checked against.

## Map 2 — Anatomy Trains (Tier 1 to Tier 3 — it varies line by line)

Thomas Myers' myofascial meridians: the body as a continuous fascial network
rather than 600 separate muscles. Force transmits *along* a line, so the place
that hurts is often not the place the strain originates.

- **Posterior chain line:** the **Superficial Back Line (SBL)** — plantar fascia
  → calves / hamstrings → erector spinae → scalp. **This line is Wilke-2016
  confirmed** (strong evidence — all 3 of 3 transitions verified). It is solid
  ground to reason from.
- **The torsion line:** the **Spiral Line** is **partially confirmed** (Tier 2 —
  5 of 9 transitions verified, 21 studies). Usable for a cross-body torsion
  pattern, but the ~4/9 unverified segments get hedged — never the full helix as
  established.
- **The honest contrast:** the **Superficial Front Line (SFL)** had *no verified
  transitions* in the same review — the genuinely unsupported line (Tier 3). It is
  kept only as the cautionary contrast. *Evidence is not uniform even within
  Anatomy Trains* — SBL confirmed (3/3), Spiral partial (5/9), SFL unsupported (0).
  When this tool reaches for an AT line, it names which tier that specific line
  sits in.
- **Claim type:** mechanical force transmission — testable, and tested with mixed
  results line-by-line.

## Map 3 — TCM / meridian (Tier 2 location-correlation; energetic theory untested)

The acupuncture-channel map. The posterior-chain channel is the **Bladder
meridian**, the longest channel, running head-to-toe down the back body.

Two layers that must never be conflated:

- **The location layer (Tier 2, real):** Langevin & Yandow (2002) found ~80% of
  acupoint / meridian intersections coincide with connective-tissue planes. The
  channels often sit *where* the fascia is. This is a genuine anatomical
  correlation — about *location*, not mechanism.
- **The energetic layer (untested tradition):** qi flow along meridians. This is
  not validated by the Langevin finding and is not claimed as fact here.
- **Cooley's emotional layer (weakest tier — inquiry only):** Bob Cooley's
  reinterpretation maps each channel to an emotional/functional theme (Bladder =
  honest support / hope). This is an *interpretive framework*, the weakest tier.
  It is offered only as a somatic-inquiry *question* — never as causation.

## Map 4 — Thai Sen lines (transmitted tradition — outside the testable frame)

The Thai lineage map: rivers of *Lom* (vital air / wind) through the body,
attributed to the tradition and carried by teachers. The posterior-chain lines:

- **Sen Kalathari** — the great integrative web; the full-body X.
- **Nanthakrawat / Kitchanna** — the back-of-leg lines running into the low back.

This map is **transmitted lineage knowledge** (teacher Pichest Boonthume's
tradition). This researcher names the lines and their *public physical territory*
and **defers all depth to the source.** It authors no new claims about what a Sen
line "means," its emotional theme, or its clinical use. (See `rules.md`, Refusal
Gate 4. For the lineage teaching itself: `gabeyoga.com/books`, `gabeyoga.com/cards`.)

## The governor — Myers' "latitude and longitude"

> Myers himself refuses to equate his myofascial meridians with acupuncture
> meridians. He calls them **lines of latitude and longitude** — two different
> coordinate systems over the same globe.

That image governs everything:

- **Latitude and longitude cross.** So do these maps — they share physical
  territory along the back of the body.
- **Latitude is not longitude.** A point can sit at a known latitude *and* a known
  longitude; that does not make the two lines the same line. The Bladder meridian
  and the SBL can run through the same corridor and remain different maps with
  different mechanisms.
- **The map is not the terrain.** All four maps describe the same body. None of
  them *is* the body, and none of them is each other.

**The discipline:** connect the maps on the terrain they share; never collapse
the coordinate systems into one. The winning claim is not "the maps are one" — it
is "the disciplined translator that knows convergence, divergence, and register."
That honesty is what earns respect from the evidence-based room and the
traditional room at the same time.
